The loop

From a recorded call to a filed bug — without leaving your product.

You already talk to customers. Klavity makes those conversations show up exactly where you work.

📝01 / EXTRACT

Drop in a transcript

Upload an interview or call. Klavity extracts each speaker into a Sim — their role, what they love, want, and hate — every insight anchored to a verbatim quote they actually said.

🧬02 / REVIEW

A Sim reviews your screen

While you browse your own app, the right Sim walks to the element it cares about, points at it, and reacts in the real customer's voice — grounded, specific, in character.

🐛03 / FILE

One click → a real ticket

Turn any reaction into a Jira, Linear, GitHub, or Plane issue — with the screenshot, the page, and the customer's quote attached. Same tracker as your manual bug reports.

Meet your Sims

Not personas on a slide. The people from your calls.

Each Sim is built from real transcripts and gets sharper every time you add another. Push them to your team so everyone ships with the same customers watching.

The arc

Snap shipped. Sims live. AutoSim shipped.

Named after Ekalavya, the self-taught master — Klavity learns your product and your customers, then acts on its own.

● Shipped

Klavity Snap

the eyes

Right-click bug reporting from any page, into any tracker. Free and open source — the wedge the rest is built on.

● Live

Klavity Sims

the voices

AI personas built from real customer interviews that review your product pages — automatically on allowlisted URLs, or on demand with one click — and file what they find.

● Shipped

Klavity AutoSim

the autopilot

Your Sims test every release — and self-heal. Deterministic zero-AI replay heals when the UI moves, and a real break files a grounded finding instead of a silent false-green.

The obvious objection

“Aren't these just made-up opinions?”

No. Every Sim reaction is anchored to a verbatim quote a real person said in a real call — Klavity verifies the quote exists in the source transcript before it ever surfaces. The Sim reads your actual screen and will even disagree with its own notes when the screen has changed. Grounded, not generated.
